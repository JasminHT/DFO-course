Page 13 – Unit 1.3 Stock Status and Reference Points

[background music begins]

[Visual: The words, “Stock Status and Reference Points” appear.]

Narrator: This figure shows how the amount of fish that can be removed from a stock might change for each stock status zone (critical, cautious, or healthy).

[Visual: The graph appears with Removal Rate on the y axis, and Stock Status on the x axis. Below the graph, a legend displays “1 Limit Reference Point; 2 Upper Stock Reference Point; 3 Removal Reference; 4 Target Reference.”]

[Visual: At the top, the graph is divided into three sections and colours; a horizontal red bar on the far left is labelled “Critical zone”, a horizontal yellow bar in the middle is labelled “Cautious Zone”, and a horizontal green bar on the far right is labelled “Healthy Zone.”]

Narrator: When a stock is in the healthy zone, the removal rate can remain constant (as you can see in 3);

[Visual: A horizontal solid line labelled 3 is displayed in the Healthy Zone section on the far right.]

The vertical dotted line, 4, shows the target reference point, where managers would generally like the stock to be.

[Visual: The horizontal line labelled 3 is intersected by a perpendicular vertical dotted line labelled 4, also in the Healthy Zone section of the graph.]

Narrator: However, when the stock enters the cautious zone, fishing must be progressively reduced to avoid reaching the Limit Reference Point or LRP. You can see the dotted line descending lower and lower until it hits the LRP, showing how fishing rates might be reduced in the cautious zone. 

[Visual: The number 1 appears on the graph at the intersection of the far left “critical zone” and middle “cautious zone” denoting the location of the Limit Reference Point. A descending diagonal dotted line labelled 2 is displayed across the middle “cautious zone” section of the graph, connecting the horizontal line in the far right “healthy zone” section to the x axis at the intersection between the middle “cautious zone” and far left “critical zone” sections.]

Narrator: Below the LRP, fishing is kept at the lowest possible level to promote stock growth and avoid serious harm to the stock. 

[Visual: A horizontal dotted line connects the right side of the far left Critical Zone section of the graph with the y axis.]

Page 4 – Unit 4.1 – Science Advisory Process Overview

[background music begins]

[Visual: The words, “Science Advisory Process Overview” appear.]

Narrator: Let’s take a closer look at the 8 steps the Canadian science Advisory Secretariat uses to determine participation, scope, and other elements in the Science Advisory Process.

[Visual: A circular figure appears (similar to a pie chart) that has 8 multi-coloured sections, each representing a step in the process. The title “CSAS Process” appears in the centre of the figure.]

[Visual: section 1 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the right half of the screen: Management sectors within the Department request science advice from the Ecosystems and Oceans Science (EOS) sector. Management sectors include: fisheries Harbour Management, aquaculture, aquatic Ecosystems (including Species at Risk).]

Narrator: Step 1. Management sectors within the Department request science advice from the Ecosystems and Oceans Science (EOS) sector. Management sectors include:

  • fisheries Harbour Management
  • aquaculture
  • aquatic Ecosystems (including Species at Risk)

[Visual: section 2 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the right half of the screen: EOS reviews requests to determine capacity to deliver.]

Narrator: Step 2. EOS reviews requests to determine capacity to deliver.

[Visual: section 3 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the right half of the screen: For selected requests, a Steering Committee is convened to guide the CSAS process. Steering Committees should include: meeting chair(s), subject matter experts,1 participant from the management sector that submitted the request, 1 participant from the CSAS office(s).]

Narrator: Step 3. For selected requests, a Steering Committee is convened to guide the CSAS process. Steering Committees should include:

  • meeting chair(s)
  • subject matter experts
  • 1 participant from the management sector that submitted the request
  • 1 participant from the CSAS office(s)

[Visual: section 3 of the circular figure continues to be highlighted. The following text appears on the right half of the screen: The Steering Committee is also responsible for identifying and inviting experts to participate in a peer review meeting. Experts may include representatives from:

  • DFO
  • other government departments
  • Indigenous Peoples
  • industry
  • academia
  • environmental non-government organizations
  • international organizations.]

Narrator: The Steering Committee is also responsible for identifying and inviting experts to participate in a peer review meeting. Experts may include representatives from:

  • DFO
  • other government departments
  • Indigenous Peoples
  • industry
  • academia
  • environmental non-government organizations
  • international organizations.

[Visual: section 4 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the right half of the screen: The Steering Committee is responsible for defining the scope of the request and developing Terms of Reference.]

Narrator:Step 4. The Steering Committee is responsible for defining the scope of the request and developing Terms of Reference.

[Visual: section 5 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the left half of the screen: Scientists collect and analyze data and prepare working papers for peer review. Working papers include technical and scientific information. They are not science advice, but contain the supporting information and analyses needed to generate advice. They do not recommend policy options]

Narrator: Step 5. Scientists collect and analyze data and prepare working papers for peer review. Working papers include technical and scientific information. They are not science advice, but contain the supporting information and analyses needed to generate advice. They do not recommend policy options.

[Visual: section 6 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the left half of the screen: Peer review meetings are where the technical review and challenge of working papers occurs. This leads to the development of consensus-based science advice. The objective of peer review meetings is to provide sound, objective, and impartial science advice. They are not consultations.]

Narrator: Step 6. Peer review meetings are where the technical review and challenge of working papers occurs. This leads to the development of consensus-based science advice. The objective of peer review meetings is to provide sound, objective, and impartial science advice. They are not consultations.

[Visual: section 7 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the left half of the screen: In some cases, a science response process may be initiated instead of a science advisory process. This is appropriate:

1. to respond to urgent and unforeseen issues

2. when a full advisory meeting is not required because the issue can be addressed through the application of an existing advisory framework. For example, a stock assessment where the peer review process has previously been used to approve methodology that are reused to update analyses.]

Narrator: Step 7. In some cases, a science response process may be initiated instead of a science advisory process. This is appropriate:

  1. to respond to urgent and unforeseen issues
  2. when a full advisory meeting is not required because the issue can be addressed through the application of an existing advisory framework. For example, a stock assessment where the peer review process has previously been used to approve methodology that are reused to update analyses.

[Visual: section 8 of the circular figure is highlighted. The following text appears on the left half of the screen: All CSAS products are available through DFO’s public-facing website.]

Narrator: Step 8. All CSAS products are available through DFO’s public-facing website.
